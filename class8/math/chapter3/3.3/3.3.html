EXERCISE 3.3 SOLUTIONS

1. Given a parallelogram ABCD. Complete each statement along with the definition or property used.

(i) AD = ...... (ii) ∠DCB = ......

(iii) OC = ...... (iv) m∠DAB + m∠CDA = ......

Properties of a parallelogram:

  • Opposite sides are equal
  • Opposite angles are equal
  • Diagonals bisect each other
  • Adjacent angles are supplementary (sum to 180°)

(i) AD = BC (Opposite sides of a parallelogram are equal)

(ii) ∠DCB = ∠DAB (Opposite angles of a parallelogram are equal)

(iii) OC = OA (Diagonals of a parallelogram bisect each other)

(iv) m∠DAB + m∠CDA = 180° (Adjacent angles in a parallelogram are supplementary)

2. Consider the following parallelograms. Find the values of the unknowns x, y, z.

(i) Solution:

In a parallelogram, adjacent angles are supplementary.

So, \( x + 100^\circ = 180^\circ \) ⇒ \( x = 80^\circ \)

Opposite angles are equal, so \( y = 100^\circ \), \( z = 80^\circ \)

(ii) Solution:

In a parallelogram, adjacent angles are supplementary.

So, \( 50^\circ + y = 180^\circ \) ⇒ \( y = 130^\circ \)

Opposite angles are equal, so \( x = y = 130^\circ \)

Also, \( z = x = 130^\circ \) (corresponding angles)

(iii) Solution:

In a parallelogram, opposite angles are equal.

So, \( x = 90^\circ \)

Also, adjacent angles are supplementary.

So, \( 90^\circ + y + 30^\circ = 180^\circ \) ⇒ \( y = 60^\circ \)

Using angle sum property in triangle: \( z + 30^\circ + 90^\circ = 180^\circ \) ⇒ \( z = 60^\circ \)

(iv) Solution:

In a parallelogram, adjacent angles are supplementary.

So, \( x + 80^\circ = 180^\circ \) ⇒ \( x = 100^\circ \)

Opposite angles are equal, so \( y = 80^\circ \)

Since \( z \) is corresponding to \( 80^\circ \), \( z = 80^\circ \)

(v) Solution:

In a parallelogram, opposite angles are equal.

So, \( y = 112^\circ \)

Also, adjacent angles are supplementary.

So, \( 40^\circ + y + z = 180^\circ \) ⇒ \( 40^\circ + 112^\circ + z = 180^\circ \) ⇒ \( z = 28^\circ \)

Since alternate angles are equal, \( x = z = 28^\circ \)

3. Can a quadrilateral ABCD be a parallelogram if:

(i) ∠D + ∠B = 180°? (ii) AB = DC = 8 cm, AD = 4 cm, BC = 4.4 cm?

(iii) ∠A = 70° and ∠C = 65°?

(i) Solution:

∠D + ∠B = 180° alone is not sufficient to prove ABCD is a parallelogram.

For a quadrilateral to be a parallelogram, both pairs of opposite angles should be equal, or both pairs of opposite sides should be equal, or diagonals should bisect each other.

Just one pair of adjacent angles being supplementary is not enough.

(ii) Solution:

AB = DC = 8 cm, AD = 4 cm, BC = 4.4 cm

In a parallelogram, opposite sides are equal.

Here, AD = 4 cm but BC = 4.4 cm, so AD ≠ BC.

Therefore, ABCD cannot be a parallelogram.

(iii) Solution:

∠A = 70° and ∠C = 65°

In a parallelogram, opposite angles are equal.

Here, ∠A ≠ ∠C (70° ≠ 65°), so ABCD cannot be a parallelogram.

4. Draw a rough figure of a quadrilateral that is not a parallelogram but has exactly two opposite angles of equal measure.

A kite is an example of a quadrilateral that is not a parallelogram but has exactly two opposite angles of equal measure.

In a kite, one pair of opposite angles are equal, but it is not a parallelogram because its sides are not parallel.

5. The measures of two adjacent angles of a parallelogram are in the ratio 3:2. Find the measure of each of the angles of the parallelogram.

Let the measures of two adjacent angles be \( 3x \) and \( 2x \).

In a parallelogram, adjacent angles are supplementary.

\( angle A + angleB = 180^\circ \)

So, \( 2x + 3x = 180^\circ \)

\( 5x = 180^\circ \)

\( x = 36^\circ \)

Therefore, the angles are \( 3x = 3 \times 36^\circ = 108^\circ \) and \( 2x = 2 \times 36^\circ = 72^\circ \)

In a parallelogram, opposite angles are equal.

So, the four angles are \( 108^\circ, 72^\circ, 108^\circ, 72^\circ \).

6. Two adjacent angles of a parallelogram have equal measure. Find the measure of each of the angles of the parallelogram.

Let each adjacent angle be \( x \).

In a parallelogram, adjacent angles are supplementary.

So, \( x + x = 180^\circ \)

\( 2x = 180^\circ \)

\( x = 90^\circ \)

Therefore, each angle of the parallelogram is \( 90^\circ \).

This is a special case of a parallelogram - a rectangle.

10. Explain how this figure is a trapezium. Which of its two sides are parallel?

A trapezium is a quadrilateral with at least one pair of parallel sides.

In the given figure, ∠M + ∠L = 180° (100° + 80° = 180°)

When the sum of co-interior angles is 180°, the lines are parallel.

So, KL is parallel to NM.

Therefore, the figure is a trapezium with sides KL and NM parallel.
