Exercise 3.4 — Solutions

Q1. State whether True or False:
(a) All rectangles are squares. → False
A square is a rectangle, but not all rectangles are squares.
(b) All rhombuses are parallelograms. → True
Rhombus has opposite sides parallel, so it’s a parallelogram.
(c) All squares are rhombuses and also rectangles. → True
Squares meet both rhombus and rectangle properties.
(d) All squares are not parallelograms. → False
Squares satisfy parallelogram properties..
(e) All kites are rhombuses. → False
Kites don’t necessarily have all sides equal.
(f) All rhombuses are kites. → True
Rhombuses have two pairs of equal adjacent sides.
(g) All parallelograms are trapeziums. → True
Both have at least one pair of parallel sides.
(h) All squares are trapeziums. → True
Squares have parallel sides, fulfilling trapezium definition.
Q2. Identify quadrilaterals with:
(a) Four sides of equal length → Rhombus, Square
(b) Four right angles → Rectangle, Square
Q3. Explain how a square is:
(i) A quadrilateral → It has four sides.
(ii) A parallelogram → Opposite sides are parallel, opposite angles equal.
(iii) A rhombus → All four sides are equal and diagonals bisect at right angles.
(iv) A rectangle → All angles are 90° and opposite sides equal.
Q4. Quadrilaterals whose diagonals:
(i) Bisect each other → Parallelogram, Rhombus, Rectangle, Square
(ii) Are perpendicular bisectors of each other → Rhombus, Square
(iii) Are equal → Rectangle, Square
Q5. Explain why a rectangle is a convex quadrilateral.
All interior angles are less than 180°. Diagonals lie inside the figure. Hence convex.
